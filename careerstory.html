Don’t be afraid to challenge yourself!

British Steel offered me a role on their Graduate Scheme but I declined their generous offer instead accepting a Management Accountant role responsible for the reporting of 2 textile plants and 3 staff, yes it was hard to begin with but you learn a lot rolling up your sleeves and getting on with the job.

2002

Milliken - European Financial Analyst

2003

Alstom Project Accountant – Virgin Pendliino

Make the most of every opportunity

As you progress in an organisation your network is increasingly important, make the most of any chance to work with others, it’s likely that that is where your next opportunity will come from

2004

Carillion – Finance Analyst

2006

Capgemini – Major Programme Controller

2008

Capgemini – Senior Financial Controller (ASPIRE) & Capgemini Leadership Academy

2011

Capgemini – Account Finance Controller (SFA)

Do the hard stuff!

The best experience is often doing the things that no one else wants to do; following a couple of years at Capgemini I earned a reputation for resolving issues on projects. When alarm bells started to ring on a project I was the one sent in to get to the bottom of issues and recommend solutions, you learn a lot by sharing your experience.

2013

Kier – Finance Controller, Environmental

2017

Kier – Finance Director, Environmental

Lastly... Be resilient!

Leadership is about inspiring people and taking them with you, resilient leaders are perceived more positively in an organisation. You will have setbacks along the way, but learn from them and recover quickly!
